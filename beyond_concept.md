<h2> <em>CONCEPT</em></h2>
	
  <p><em>Beyond</em> is multichannel installation produced in collaboration with visual artist <a href="http://www.ibdesigner.com">Ian Bilson</a>. Consisting of a spherical geodesic structure and containing 40 loud speakers, it builds on research conducted for <a href="dome.html"><em>Dome</em></a> (2012) and its <em>raison d'etre</em> is similar: the  speakers are conceived collectively as a single sound-producing unit,  accommodating the  detailed spatial construction of sonic images over the surfaces of the domes. </p>
	<center><img src="images/Beyond/BeyondDiagram.jpg" alt="Beyond: Diagram" width="457" height="500"/></center>
 
	
	<p>As with <em>Dome</em>, the structure of the installation is designed to be unenclosed and thus acoustically transparent, enabling a listener within to experience the soundscape beyond the playing loudspeakers (ideally a park or other outdoor public space) as an extension of that presented by the constructed speaker-space itself; indeed, sounds from loudspeakers may be indistinguishable from those coming from beyond. Thus, while the sphere provides a sense of enclosure and safety, it is paradoxically designed to encourage a much wider awareness of space (and place), affording the listener an experience akin to Mallarm&eacute;&rsquo;s notion of &lsquo;transparent prolongation&rsquo;: a listening-through of the constructed land(sound)scape into the already-there. </p>
	<p>Different sound environments presented within the sphere, and    different contexts in which it is presented, invite different 'skins'/facades for the structure. The aim was    to make the structure sculpturally appealing&mdash;eye-catching and compelling&mdash;to encourage members of the public to    explore it.	</p>
