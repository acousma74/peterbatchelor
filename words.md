Batchelor, P. (2015). ‘Acousmatic approaches to the construction of image and space in sound art’, *Organised Sound*, 20(2), pp. 148–159.  
Available at: [resources/BatchelorP(2015)_AcousmaticApproachesToTheConstructionOfImageInSoundArt.pdf](resources/BatchelorP(2015)_AcousmaticApproachesToTheConstructionOfImageInSoundArt.pdf)

<small>
This article considers ideas of image and space as they apply to acousmatic music and sound art, establishing overlaps and compatibilities which are often overlooked in the tendency to treat these genres as incompatible. Two issues are foregrounded: compositional concerns (particularly mimesis, image construction, and what is termed ‘ephemeral narrative’) and presentational strategies (especially multichannel speaker deployment).

Drawing on a range of works, the article introduces the author’s *GRIDs* project—a series of multichannel sound sculptures arranged in geometric arrays of many (in some cases hundreds of) loudspeakers. These systems enable the creation of highly intricate spatial sound environments—fabricated landscapes—emerging from an acousmatic compositional aesthetic.

The article concludes by considering how listeners engage with constructed image space in these installations, particularly in relation to their visual and environmental context, and how this differs from acousmatic listening in concert settings.
</small>
