Beyond

for 40 channel geodesic sphere • 2014+

OVERVIEW • RESEARCH • DOCUMENTATION (white | willow | fire)

 

RESEARCH

The research for Beyond explored the following themes from the GRIDs research questions:

Geometric speaker arrangements:
 The dense geometric multichannel configuration is in this case derived from the aggregated triadic arrangements of speakers surrounding each geodesic 'panel' of the sphere. As with Cascade (2018) and a previous work Studies on Canvas (2004), the speakers are conceived collectively as a single sound-producing unit, accommodating the detailed spatial construction of sonic images over the surfaces of the speaker arrangement. This was in order to accommodate landscape-development strategies in common with those found elsewhere within acousmatic compositional practice.

Affordable construction:
 The spheres are constructed from affordable materials. Hazel or dowling rods attached via plastic connector 'spades' are used for the structures, making them light and portable. 3" speaker drivers housed within plastic 'tupperware' containers are powered by kit amps and driven by aggregated MOTU828 sound cards (see also Batchelor 2019b).

Extending acousmatic compositional possibilities:
 The close positioning of speakers in spherical arrangement affords increased spatial detail in comparison with larger speaker setups, along with a more individualised, intimate listening experience. Compositional strategies afforded by the arrangement can be found in Documentation along with descriptions of each compositional outcome (see also Batchelor 2015):

Intimacy
 The listening experience is made more intimate through the close positioning of speakers in spherical arrangement, which affords increased spatial detail in comparison with larger speaker setups habitually used in the presentation of acousmatic composition. This in turn increases a sense of tactility and immediacy of the sounds. The 2m diameter sphere lends a sense of enclosure and safety, along with a more individualised listening experience (Batchelor 2019: 313-5).

Encouraging closer environmental listening:
 The structure of the installation is designed to be unenclosed and thus acoustically transparent, enabling a listener within to experience the soundscape beyond the playing loudspeakers (ideally a park or other outdoor public space) as an extension of that presented by the constructed speaker-space itself; indeed, sounds from loudspeakers may be indistinguishable from those coming from beyond. Thus, while the sphere provides a sense of enclosure and safety, it is paradoxically designed to encourage a much wider awareness of space (and place), affording the listener an experience akin to Mallarmés notion of transparent prolongation: a listening-through of the constructed land(sound)scape into the already-there (ibid). This characteristics was explored in particular in Beyond: Willow.
