<h2> <em>AUDIO</em></h2>
	
  <p>This page contains documentation of the audio compositions presented over the Beyond structure. </p>
  <ul>
    <li>as binaural renderings (on this page)</li>
    <li>as ambisonic simulation (constructed in MaxMSP) </li>
  </ul>
  <p>A touchscreen was provided in each sphere, presenting eight audio scenes from which a visitor could choose ( a pre-mixed version was presented without the touchscreen where security at a given location represented a concern). The scenes were chosen such that they would complement the materials of the geodesic sphere thematically, or the context in which they were presented:</p>
  <ul>
    <li><a href="beyond_white.html">Beyond: White</a> contained  sound materials alluding to water and organic sounds (in keeping with its visual associations with the 'viral'); </li>
      <li><a href="beyond_fire.html">Beyond: Fire</a> contained sound materials alluding to fire;</li>
      <li><a href="beyond_willow.html">Beyond: Willow</a> contained natural environments that, played in the outdoor spaces, would lead at times to a sense of source ambiguity (i.e.  whether some sounds originated from recorded materials presented over the speakers (hearing the real thing) or came from beyond the speakers (i.e. really hearing the thing) (<a href="http://www.ems-network.org/IMG/pdf_BatchelorEMS07.pdf">Batchelor 2007</a>)</li>
  </ul>
  <p>Once initiated, scenes played on loop until further scenes were triggered.</p>

This composition seeks to explore enclosure vs transparency of the sphere, inside and outside regarding the origins of the sounds, and real-world vs &lsquo;composed&rsquo; sonic constructions.</p>
